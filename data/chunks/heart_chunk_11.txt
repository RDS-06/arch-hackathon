5. 
CHRONIC STABLE ANGINA  
 
5.1 
CLINICAL PRESENTATION  
 
Character: 
usually described more often as a discomfort, pressure, heaviness, 
or squeezing sensation and less often as a pain. Sometimes 
described as a burning or sharp sensation. 
 
Location: 
substernal area, precordium or epigastrium with radiation to the 
left arm, jaw or neck, less commonly felt only in radiation area and 
not in the chest. 
 
Precipitation:  often provoked by exertion, cold weather, eating, smoking and 
strong emotions.  Relieved by rest, removal of provoking factors, 
or sublingual nitrates. 
 
 
Duration: 
  few minutes usually, rarely more than 4-5 minutes. 
 
History: 
description of the chest discomfort and its relationship to activity.
