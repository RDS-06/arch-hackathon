If pain is present, nitroglycerin tablets should be given sublingually, with 
subsequent further sublingual, transdermal, or intravenous doses as necessary. 
 
If pain is not completely relieved with nitrates, intravenous opiates such as 
morphine sulphate should be considered. 
 
If oesophageal or gastric pain is suspected, an oral antacid can be given as well. 
Heart rate, blood pressure, and rhythm should be monitored closely.
