Overt behavioural change within the past six months characterises the action 
stage, which is highly unstable and where the greatest risk for relapse may occur. 
The criterion for achieving this stage is to have actually changed behaviour, eg. 
Stopped smoking, as opposed to reducing smoking or switching brand to lower 
tar, etc., During this period most of the processes of change come into play and 
the intrapersonal (eg. Perceived self-efficacy), interpersonal (e.g. social support)
