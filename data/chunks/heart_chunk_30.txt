Nitrates 
 
Nitrates dilate epicardial coronary arteries and arterioles and reduce cardiac 
preload.  They also relieve coronary spasm and dynamic stenoses, especially at 
epicardial sites.  Their use is associated with reflex tachycardia, an effect that may 
increase myocardial oxygen demand.  This response may be blunted by the 
concomitant administration of β-blockers or calcium antagonists, such as 
diltiazem or verapamil, which slow conduction.
