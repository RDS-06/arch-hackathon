Antiplatelet agents 
 
 
Aspirin (75-150 mg) exerts its antithrombotic effect by inhibiting cyclo-
oxygenase and synthesis of platelet thromboxane A2. It is effective in preventing 
first heart attacks, improving mortality in acute coronary syndromes, and reducing 
adverse cardiovascular events in patients with stable angina pectoris.  Aspirin 
should therefore be considered as first line therapy in all patients with chronic 
ischaemic heart disease.  In patients with absolute aspirin contraindications 
alternative antiplatelet regimes such as dipyridamole (Persantine), ticlopidine 
(Ticlid), glycoprotein 11b/111a receptor antagonists may be considered, although 
the clinical data supporting their use in angina pectoris is less established.
