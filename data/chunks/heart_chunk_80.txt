6.2.2 Examination and Investigations 
 
1. 
Physical Examination 
 
The rapid, risk-stratification orientated physical examination should be directed 
towards identification of: 
1. haemodynamic instability, and 
2. pulmonary congestion 
 
New systolic murmurs are particularly important, suggesting the presence of 
ischaemic mitral regurgitation (due to papillary muscle dysfunction or more 
dramatically due to papillary muscle rupture) or an acquired ventricular septal 
defect (due to rupture of the infarcted area of the septum)
