The prognosis after acute myocardial infarction is related to four main factors: 
 
• Extent of left ventricular dysfunction, including degree of left ventricular 
dilation 
• Presence of residual ischemia 
• Degree of electrical instability of the myocardium 
• Progression of coronary atherosclerosis 
 
A risk stratification should be evaluated in each patient. Noninvasive evaluation 
of low-risk patients includes submaximal or  symptom-limited stress ECG at 4 to 
6 days or 10 to 14 days, respectively. For prognostic assessment, after 
mobilization, ie after 1 week, standard exercise testing should be performed to 
assess
