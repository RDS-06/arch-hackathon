Obesity 
 
 
Obesity is a common condition associated with an increased risk for coronary 
artery disease and mortality.  Obesity is also associated  with and contributes to 
other coronary risk factors, including high blood pressure, glucose intolerance, 
low levels of HDL cholesterol, and elevated triglycerides.  Thus, much of the 
increased CAD risk associated with obesity is mediated by these factors.  Weight 
reduction in obese patients with coronary disease can likely reduce the risk of 
future events because weight reduction will reduce other modifiable risk factors 
and reduce the increased myocardial oxygen demand imposed by obesity.  Weight
