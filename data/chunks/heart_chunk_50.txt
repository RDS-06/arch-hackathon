6.1.3 Differential Diagnosis 
 
The diagnosis of non-ST  elevation ACS should be  suspected in any patient who  
presents with new or worsening  ischaemic chest pain consistent  with angina, 
with ECG  abnormalities suggestive of  ischaemia but without ST  elevation.
