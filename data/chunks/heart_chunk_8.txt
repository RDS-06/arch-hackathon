Acute coronary syndromes share a common pathophysiology: acute rupture 
(fissuration or ulceration) of a lipid-rich intracoronary atheromatous plaque, with 
subsequent mobilisation of a sequence of inflammatory and thrombotic cascades 
culminating in the formation of thrombus.

increased neurohormonal tone.  Certain plaques are more susceptible to acute
