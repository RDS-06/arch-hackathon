For bradyarrhythmia and  heart block, either intravenous atropine or temporary 
transvenous (or, in some cases, transcutaneous) pacing is recommended. 
Indications  for pacing are symptomatic bradycardia (less than 50 beats per 
minute) with symptoms of hypotension not responding to atropine;  asystole; 
bilateral bundle branch block; newly developed or indeterminate bifascicular 
block with first-degree AV block; second-degree  symptomatic or Mobitz type II 
AV block, advanced (complete) AV-block; and incessant ventricular tachycardia, 
which requires atrial or  ventricular overdrive pacing.
