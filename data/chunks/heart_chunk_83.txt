ST-wave elevation may persist in cases of left ventricular dyskinesis or aneurysm. 
The electrocardiographic leads not representing the infarcted area may show 
inverse, reciprocal ST-segment alterations.  
 
A new left bundle branch block  may be present and, if it persists, usually 
indicates large anterior wall infarction. 
 
The diagnosis of acute infarction in patients with acute chest pain who present 
with left  bundle branch block is highly probable when there is ST-segment 
elevation of 1 mm or more concordant with the QRS complex, ST-segment
