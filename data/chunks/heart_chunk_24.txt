Short-acting dihydropyridine calcium antagonists have the potential to enhance 
the risk of adverse cardiac events and should be avoided.  Long-acting calcium 
antagonists of the dihydro-and nondihydropyridine class relieve angina and are 
appropriate initial therapy in patients with contraindications to β-blockers.  They 
can also be substituted for β-blockers in patients who develop unacceptable side 
effects or can be used in combination with β-blockers when initial β-blockers 
therapy is unsuccessful.

preload.  They also relieve coronary spasm and dynamic stenoses, especially at 
epicardial sites.  Their use is associated with reflex tachycardia, an effect that may
