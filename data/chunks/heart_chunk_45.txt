Tachycardia or bradycardia, as well as extremes of blood pressure, may be 
present, but vital signs are often normal. 
 
Heart failure or valvular regurgitation are unusual acute consequences of non-ST 
elevation ACS, but may be concurrently present in patients with underlying 
cardiac disease. 
 
Signs of peripheral vascular disease, such as carotid bruits or diminished 
extremity pulses, should increase the suspicion of atherosclerotic coronary 
disease.  
 
Physical examination is perhaps most useful to diagnose non-ischaemic causes 
of pain, such as costochondritis, pneumonia, obstructive pulmonary disease, 
pericarditis, aortic dissection, upper gastrointestinal disease etc.
