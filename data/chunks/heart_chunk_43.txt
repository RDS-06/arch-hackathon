Exercise 
  
 
Exercise training improves blood pressure, glucose control, lipid profile, exercise 
endurance, and objective measures of ischaemia.  In addition, exercise training 
may lead to changes in weight and a sense of well-being.  For these reasons, a 
regular exercise program should be part of a multifactorial intervention program 
in patients with stable chronic ischaemic heart disease.
