5.5 
FOLLOW-UP 
 
 
Patients who are successfully treated for chronic ischaemic heart disease should 
have follow-up evaluation every 4 to 12 months.  A more precise interval cannot 
be recommended because many factors influence the length of follow-up period.  
Five questions must be answered regularly during the follow-up evaluation: 
 
(1) 
Has the patient decreased the level of physical activity? 
(2) 
Have the patient’s anginal symptoms increased in frequency and become 
more severe since the last visit? 
(3) 
How well is the patient tolerating therapy? 
(4) 
How successful has the patient been in reducing modifiable risk factors
