maintaining normal resting coronary blood flow.  Consequently, at rest, most 
patients with significant coronary artery stenosis obstructions have no ischaemia 
and therefore no angina.  During exercise, however, the capacity of coronary 
arterioles to dilate further is limited, and the myocardial oxygen demand soon 
outstrips the supply, resulting in ischaemia, followed usually by angina.
