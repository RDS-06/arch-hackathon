The possible triggers of acute plaque rupture are: sheer stress, inflammation and 
increased neurohormonal tone.  Certain plaques are more susceptible to acute 
rupture than others – for example those which are eccentric, with a lipid-rich 
vulnerable core, a high content of active inflammatory cells, and a thin fibrous 
cap.  Rupture of the plaque (ulceration/fissuration), with exposure of the highly 
thrombogenic contents to the bloodstream, initiates a cascade of cytokine release,
