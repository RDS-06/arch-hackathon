5.4.2 Education and Risk Factor Reduction 
 
 
Identification and treatment of coronary disease risk factors in patients with 
angina is necessary for optimal secondary prevention of chronic ischaemic heart 
disease.  Established and modifiable coronary disease risk factors, such as 
cigarette smoking, hypertension, diabetes mellitus, and hyperlipidemia, are 
common in patients with chronic ischaemic heart disease.  These risk factors are 
readily amenable to modification, and their treatment can affect clinical outcome 
favourably. 
 
 
Studies show that the risk of death from CAD is inversely proportional to 
education.  It is therefore important for the physician to assess the patient’s 
baseline understanding of his or her condition and to appropriately educate the 
patient and his or her family on the pathology and pathophysiology of the disease, 
prognosis of condition, and treatment and risk factor reduction options.
