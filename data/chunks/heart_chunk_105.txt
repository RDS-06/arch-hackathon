6.2.5 Follow-up and Rehabilitation 
 
1. 
Follow-up 
 
In all patients following myocardial infarction without contraindications, long-
term treatment with aspirin and β-adrenergic blockers is recommended . 
 
Recurrent angina should be treated with intravenous nitroglycerin, aspirin, 
heparin,  analgesics, and β-blockers. 
 
ACE inhibitors should be a part of secondary prevention in patients with left  
ventricular dysfunction. 
 
Calcium antagonists are not routinely recommended after infarction but may be 
selectively prescribed in patients with specific indications such as hypertension or
