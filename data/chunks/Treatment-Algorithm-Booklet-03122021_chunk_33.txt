 Chest X-ray
Scrub typhus: Pneumonia is most common systemic involvement. Bilateral opacities 
progressing to ARDS may be seen in scrub typhus, leptospirosis, and occasionally in 
malaria. Pneumonia occurs occasionally in enteric fever. Pleural effusion occasional in

Others: Bilateral nodular opacities or upper lobe cavitating pneumonia in melioidosis
