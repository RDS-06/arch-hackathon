INDICATIONS FOR REFERRAL TO CARDIOLOGIST 
 
 
In general, most patients with chronic stable angina initially present to their  
primary care physician.  The preliminary diagnosis, risk stratification, medical 
management, risk factor modification, and patient education are performed under 
direction of the primary care physician.  Patients with worsening anginal 
symptoms (Canadian Cardiovascular Society Angina Class III or IV) or 
presenting with any acute coronary syndrome should be referred to a cardiologist.
