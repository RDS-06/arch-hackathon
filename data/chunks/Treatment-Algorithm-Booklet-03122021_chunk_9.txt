Unmet Needs in Management of Acute Fever in Primary Care
Acute fever has myriad causes. The diverse infectious etiologies often overlap and present a challenge to 
the treating physicians.
The majority of patients with 
acute fever present at clinics with 
nonspecific symptoms, such as 
low-grade fever, general malaise, 
headache, arthralgia, myalgia, and 
rash with or without a focal point of 
infection.4,5,13
