DEFINITION OF RISK FACTOR 
 
  
The term risk factor describes those “characteristics found in healthy individuals 
to be independently related to the subsequent occurrence of coronary heart disease 
and, where modifiable, to be reversible.’ For example, cholesterol is an 
independent risk factor for coronary heart disease.   Epidemiological and 
evidence-based studies have shown that cholesterol lowering by one percent is 
associated with a decrease in the risk of coronary heart disease of about 2%.
